# Evidence Integrity Profile Guidance

**Status:** Non-normative v0.5.x follow-up guidance

## Purpose

This document defines non-normative guidance for relating Evidence Integrity Levels to high-impact and audit-grade assessment contexts in AAEF.

It addresses follow-up work from the v0.5.0 Evidence Integrity Levels, Risk-Proportional Evidence, and Tamper-Evident Evidence planning material.

The purpose is to clarify when stronger evidence integrity should be considered for higher-risk, higher-impact, or audit-grade agentic actions.

This document does not add new control IDs, change assessment profiles, change the evidence schema, or change the v0.4.1 control and assessment baseline.

## Core Principle

Evidence integrity expectations should be proportional to action impact, assurance need, and review context.

Low-risk actions should not require unnecessarily heavyweight evidence integrity.

High-impact, cross-boundary, externally effective, privileged, destructive, financial, or audit-grade actions may require stronger evidence integrity to support reconstruction, review, and accountability.

## Evidence Integrity and Evidence Depth

AAEF distinguishes evidence depth from evidence integrity.

| Dimension | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Evidence depth | How much relevant evidence exists for the action, authorization, execution, and review context. |
| Evidence integrity | How resistant the evidence is to tampering, omission, replay, self-reporting bias, or later alteration. |

An action may have deep evidence that is weakly protected.

An action may have tamper-evident evidence that is incomplete.

Assessment should consider both.

## Planning-Level Integrity Bands

The following bands are non-normative planning categories.

They are intended to help reviewers reason about evidence integrity expectations.

| Band | Description | Typical interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| EI-0 | No meaningful evidence integrity | Evidence is absent, unverifiable, or purely model/self-report based. |
| EI-1 | Basic operational record | Evidence exists but may be mutable, local, or weakly attributable. |
| EI-2 | Attributable system evidence | Evidence is generated by a system component with clear attribution and action linkage. |
| EI-3 | Corroborated or independently generated evidence | Evidence is generated or corroborated by policy, enforcement, backend, workflow, or evidence components outside the model runtime. |
| EI-4 | Tamper-evident or write-restricted evidence | Evidence includes append-only, write-restricted, signed, hash-linked, externally timestamped, or similarly tamper-evident properties. |
| EI-5 | Audit-grade evidence integrity | Evidence is strong enough for high-assurance review, incident reconstruction, or formal audit support, with clear lineage, retention, and tamper-evidence expectations. |

These bands are guidance only.

They are not required schema values in this document.

## Risk-Proportional Profile Guidance

The following table provides planning-level guidance for relating action context to evidence integrity.

| Action context | Suggested integrity posture | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Low-risk local action | EI-1 or EI-2 may be sufficient | Avoid unnecessary evidence burden. |
| Internal reversible action | EI-2 may be sufficient | Stronger evidence may be needed if privileged or sensitive. |
| Sensitive data access | EI-2 or EI-3 recommended | Evidence should show authorization and data scope. |
| Privileged tool use | EI-3 recommended | Enforcement or backend corroboration is usually important. |
| Externally effective action | EI-3 recommended, EI-4 considered | External effect increases reconstruction need. |
| Financial or resource-consuming action | EI-3 recommended, EI-4 considered | Budget, approval, and execution evidence should be correlated. |
| Production change | EI-3 or EI-4 recommended | Change evidence should support rollback and incident review. |
| Destructive or irreversible action | EI-4 recommended | Tamper-evident evidence may be important. |
| Cross-agent or cross-domain delegation | EI-3 or EI-4 recommended | Delegation chain evidence should be reconstructable. |
| High-impact action | EI-4 recommended, EI-5 considered | Stronger evidence may be needed depending on assurance claim. |
| Audit-grade assessment context | EI-4 or EI-5 considered | Evidence integrity should support independent review. |

The table is not a normative pass/fail standard.

It provides candidate guidance for future assessment profile refinement.

## High-Impact Evidence Expectations

For high-impact actions, evidence should support review of:

- who or what principal authorized the action;
- what action was authorized;
- what action was actually executed;
- whether the action matched the authorized scope;
- which policy or approval context applied;
- which enforcement point permitted or denied execution;
- what evidence was generated independently of the model output;
- whether evidence was preserved after the action;
- whether evidence can be correlated across authorization, dispatch, execution, and outcome.

High-impact actions may require evidence integrity stronger than ordinary operational logging.

## Audit-Grade Evidence Expectations

Audit-grade contexts may require evidence that is:

- action-bound;
- principal-bound;
- authorization-bound;
- execution-bound;
- timestamped or sequence-preserved;
- attributable to system components;
- independently generated or corroborated;
- protected from unauthorized modification;
- retained for a defined period;
- reviewable by a party other than the agent runtime;
- able to support incident reconstruction.

Audit-grade evidence does not necessarily require a single technology.

It requires evidence properties that support reviewability, accountability, and integrity.

## Candidate Assessment Profile Considerations

Future assessment profile work may consider whether profiles should include evidence integrity expectations.

Candidate profile inputs include:

- action impact level;
- evidence depth;
- evidence integrity band;
- presence of independent or corroborating evidence;
- presence of tamper-evident evidence;
- action reversibility;
- external effect;
- financial or resource impact;
- cross-agent delegation;
- cross-domain action;
- production environment;
- sensitive data access;
- human approval quality;
- incident reconstruction need.

These are candidates for future profile refinement only.

## Candidate Evidence Questions

Reviewers may ask:

- Is the evidence generated only by the model or agent runtime?
- Is the evidence generated by a policy, enforcement, workflow, backend, or evidence component?
- Can the evidence be correlated to the specific action?
- Can the evidence prove that the executed action matched the authorized action?
- Can the evidence show whether approval, reauthorization, or delegation was required?
- Can the evidence be modified or deleted without detection?
- Can earlier denial, refusal, or failed validation evidence be overwritten by later success?
- Is evidence preserved when the action fails, is denied, is refused, or is escalated?
- Is the evidence sufficient for incident reconstruction?

## Performance and Storage Tradeoffs

Higher evidence integrity may create overhead.

Potential overhead includes:

- storage volume;
- latency;
- indexing cost;
- retention cost;
- cryptographic signing or verification cost;
- operational complexity;
- privacy or data minimization concerns;
- reviewer burden;
- SIEM or audit pipeline cost.

AAEF should not require maximum evidence integrity for every action.

Evidence integrity should be risk-proportional.

## Evidence Minimization

Risk-proportional evidence also means avoiding unnecessary collection.

Low-risk actions may not need high-integrity evidence if:

- the action is reversible;
- the action has no external effect;
- no sensitive data is accessed;
- no high-impact tool is invoked;
- no cross-agent or cross-domain authority is involved;
- incident reconstruction need is low;
- the evidence burden would exceed the assurance value.

However, low-risk classification should not be used to hide high-impact effects.

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Risk |
| --- | --- |
| Treating model explanations as audit-grade evidence | Model output may explain but does not prove authority, execution, or integrity. |
| Treating mutable logs as sufficient for high-impact actions | Evidence may be altered or deleted after the action. |
| Collecting excessive evidence for all actions | Creates privacy, storage, cost, and review burden. |
| Ignoring denied or refused action evidence | Incident reconstruction may miss attempted bypasses. |
| Preserving only successful execution evidence | Earlier failure, refusal, or escalation may be lost. |
| Treating evidence depth as evidence integrity | Many weak records do not necessarily create strong evidence. |
| Treating tamper-evident storage as complete evidence | Integrity does not prove completeness or relevance. |
| Using audit-grade claims without retention and reviewability | Evidence may be strong technically but unusable in assessment. |

## Relationship to Tamper-Evident Evidence

Tamper-evident evidence is one way to improve evidence integrity.

It may be appropriate for selected high-impact, audit-grade, cross-agent, cross-domain, financial, privileged, destructive, or externally effective contexts.

Tamper-evident evidence should not be treated as a universal requirement for all actions.

Detailed tamper-evident evidence guidance is tracked separately in the v0.5.x follow-up work for selected contexts.

## Non-Goals

This document does not:

- change assessment profiles;
- add new control IDs;
- change the evidence schema;
- change evidence examples;
- change testing procedures;
- require tamper-evident evidence for all actions;
- mandate a specific logging, SIEM, cryptographic, storage, or audit technology;
- claim that evidence integrity alone proves authorization or execution correctness.

Any normative incorporation must be handled through a later PR that explicitly updates the relevant control, testing, evidence, assessment, schema, mapping, or release artifacts.
