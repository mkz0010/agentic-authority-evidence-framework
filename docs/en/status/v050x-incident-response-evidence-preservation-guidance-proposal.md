## Guidance Objective

The objective is to define how evidence relevant to agentic actions should be preserved when an incident, suspected misuse, unauthorized action, or evidence-integrity concern is identified.

The guidance is intended to support:

- incident reconstruction;
- review of high-impact agentic actions;
- verification of authorization and execution linkage;
- detection of tampering, deletion, replay, truncation, and selective omission;
- preservation of evidence trust limitations;
- separation of model-generated summaries from trusted evidence.

## Preservation Trigger Conditions

Evidence preservation should be considered when any of the following occur:

- suspected unauthorized high-impact action;
- failed or unavailable evidence integrity verification;
- missing or inconsistent authorization evidence;
- approval-to-execution mismatch;
- suspected evidence tampering, deletion, replay, truncation, or selective omission;
- suspicious override, reauthorization, non-execution, or revocation behavior;
- incident response investigation involving agentic tool execution;
- dispute over whether an agentic action was authorized, dispatched, executed, blocked, or partially executed.

## Evidence to Preserve

Preservation should include action-bound evidence where available.

Candidate evidence includes:

| Evidence type | Preservation purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Authorization decision artifact | Prove what was authorized, by what policy, and under what context. |
| Approval workflow record | Prove approval source, approver view, approved scope, and approval timing. |
| Tool dispatch record | Prove what action was dispatched and through which enforcement point. |
| Backend execution record | Prove what was actually executed by the system of record. |
| Non-execution or blocked-action record | Prove that an action was denied, blocked, revoked, or not executed. |
| Reauthorization or override record | Prove changes to authority, approval, or policy state. |
| Evidence integrity proof | Support verification of evidence completeness and tamper-evidence. |
| External anchor or timestamp | Support independent timing or anchoring of evidence. |
| Evidence trust limitation | Preserve known limitations in completeness, source independence, or verifiability. |
| Corroborating logs | Support reconstruction across enforcement, backend, and evidence systems. |

## Preservation Requirements Candidate

A later guidance or assessment refinement may recommend that implementations preserve:

- original evidence records;
- referenced evidence packages;
- verification results;
- failed verification results;
- evidence integrity proof references;
- evidence trust limitations;
- related authorization, approval, dispatch, execution, and non-execution records;
- system time and clock source context where relevant;
- policy version, enforcement point, and evidence store identifiers;
- chain-of-custody or reviewer access records where available.

This proposal intentionally avoids making all of these mandatory for every system.

## Trust Boundary Considerations

Incident-response evidence preservation should distinguish:

- evidence generated by trusted enforcement components;
- evidence generated by backend systems of record;
- evidence generated by evidence stores or logging pipelines;
- model-generated summaries;
- agent runtime self-reports;
- implementation self-reports;
- screenshots or exported artifacts without independent integrity protection.

Model-generated summaries may assist review, but should not replace trusted evidence records.

## Preservation Actions

Candidate preservation actions include:

1. Freeze or snapshot relevant evidence packages.
2. Preserve original records before enrichment or normalization.
3. Preserve references to external anchors, signatures, hashes, or immutable storage objects.
4. Preserve failed, partial, unavailable, or not-checked verification states.
5. Preserve evidence limitations rather than overwriting them during investigation.
6. Preserve authorization, approval, dispatch, execution, and non-execution linkage.
7. Restrict deletion, redaction, compaction, or retention expiry while incident review is active.
8. Record reviewer access, export, and handoff where appropriate.

## Anti-Patterns

The guidance should warn against:

- relying only on model-generated incident summaries;
- replacing raw evidence with normalized summaries;
- overwriting failed verification results after reprocessing;
- deleting non-execution, denial, revocation, or override records;
- preserving only favorable evidence;
- treating unverifiable screenshots as equivalent to trusted evidence;
- exporting evidence without preserving proof references;
- allowing retention expiry during active incident review;
- mixing investigation notes with original evidence without clear separation.
