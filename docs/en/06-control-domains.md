# 06. Control Domains

AAEF v0.1 defines the following initial control domains.

## AAEF-GOV: Governance

Establish responsibility, scope, risk classification, ownership, and policy expectations for agentic AI systems.

## AAEF-ID: Agent Identity

Ensure that agents and agent instances are uniquely identifiable and verifiable.

## AAEF-PRN: Principal Binding

Ensure that agent actions are bound to a human, organization, system, or upstream agent principal.

## AAEF-DEL: Delegation and Authority

Ensure that authority grants are explicit, constrained, attenuated, time-bounded, and revocable.

## AAEF-AUZ: Action Authorization

Ensure that attempted actions are evaluated at the point of execution.

## AAEF-TOOL: Tool and Action Boundary

Ensure that tools and external actions are governed by policy-enforced boundaries.

## AAEF-MEM: Memory and Retrieval

Ensure that memory, retrieval, and contextual data are governed by trust, provenance, and retention controls.

## AAEF-EVD: Evidence and Auditability

Ensure that high-impact agentic actions generate structured, reviewable, and verifiable evidence.

## AAEF-HUM: Human Oversight

Ensure that human approval, review, escalation, and accountability are applied based on risk.

## AAEF-RES: Response and Revocation

Ensure that agent trust, delegated authority, credentials, tool access, memory access, and downstream delegations can be revoked or isolated.

## Domain Design

Each control domain may include:

- requirements,
- objectives,
- applicability criteria,
- testing procedures,
- evidence examples,
- assurance level mapping,
- and relationship to threats.
